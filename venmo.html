DESIGN GOALS

    The overall goals were to:

  • Make Venmo's mobile app short, adaptable, and fast.
  • Let the users experience the core functions without obstacles, and open the gap at the same time to make them realize the superiority on the peer to peer services platform. (Activation)
  • Impress the users and inspire them with the idea of “Just Venmo Me.”
  • If I worked on the team, keeping the app from current rates in App Store could be one of the measurable goals, another measurement could be an improvement impacts on the Millennials’ daily life.

OBJECTIVE

Who are the target users of Venmo's mobile app?

  • Venmo helps people easily transfer money to another, split the bills with friends, and see friends transactions status. Users who use Venmo’s mobile app can split the restaurant bills or rideshare bills among friends without the need to deal with physical cash. This is extremely handy in today’s modern society, more so than people realize it to be.

Reference: http://www.adweek.com/digital/venmo-wants-to-turn-its-brand-into-a-verb-just-like-google/

What is the difference between Venmo and Messenger pay?

  • Both of them are the pretty popular peer-to-peer payment services platform, “Just Venmo me” Venmo wants to turn its brand into a verb, with each payment the use of emojis or short cheeky memos follow, making it a social media platform where likes or comments can follow on each transaction. Users can add friends and also see a feed of their transactions.  Messenger pay feature is available in the Facebook Messenger app and requires a debit card (with PIN-based protection) on both ends of the transaction.

Reference: https://www.fastcompany.com/40512175/cashless-payment-apps-the-good-the-bad-the-ugly
